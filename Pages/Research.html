02 : Research

My research interests are broad, spanning everything from melt generation to lunar volatiles. Here, I summarise the research problems I am currently involved in:

Nanoanalysis of Sublimates on Pristine Lunar Orange Glass Beads

Analysis of lunar glass beads demonstrates that at least part of the lunar mantle retains significant volatile contents These beads were most likely formed in fire-fountaining volcanic eruptions, a process in which much of the original volatile contents of their parent melts was lost. However, some some of the volatiles desublimated out of the volcanic gas cloud and onto the surfaces of the beads, and are now present as 20-50nm thick coatings.

I am using tools including NanoSIMS, EDS, and TEM to investigate elemental and isotopic variations in the sublimate coatings with the goal of constraining conditions within the volcanic gas cloud and the volatile contents of the moon.

Non-Equilibrium Degassing of Noble Gases in Magmas

High 3He/4He ratios in Ocean Island Basalts (OIBs) are viewed as evidence for an undegassed primitive mantle source being sampled by deep-rooted mantle plumes. However, this model does not explain why Helium concentrations and elemental ratios of He/Ar and He/Ne in OIBs are an order of magnitude lower than in Mid-Ocean Ridge Basalts (MORBs). This is known as the 'helium concentration paradox'. Gonnermann and Mukhopadhyay (2007) invoke disequilibrium degassing as the solution to this paradox, and I am investigating this further using a bubble dynamics model based on a lattice Boltzmann method for free surface flows.
